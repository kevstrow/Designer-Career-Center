AI-Assisted Job Search Strategies

Use AI to summarize a company’s technical focus and culture Ask for recent projects, vessel types, markets served, and how design, production, and operations interact
Use AI to generate advanced job search strings Example: “marine designer” AND (AutoCAD OR piping) AND shipyard
Paste job postings into AI to extract required skills Helps target resumes without rewriting everything
Ask AI to rank jobs by fit Based on skills, location, and experience level
Generate cover-letter drafts, not final versions AI provides structure — credibility is still yours
Practice technical interview explanations Especially “walk me through your design” questions
Do NOT submit AI text verbatim Generic language is an immediate red flag to recruiters

What Hiring Managers Actually Look For

Clear, readable technical drawings Proper layers, lineweights, dimensions, and annotations matter more than flashy visuals
Ability to explain design decisions Why something was routed, spaced, or supported — not just that it was drawn
Comfort reading existing drawings Most junior work starts by modifying or interpreting legacy drawings
Evidence of learning speed Employers expect gaps — they care how quickly you close them
Professional communication Clear emails, responding to markups properly, and asking good questions
Understanding the company’s role Design office, shipyard, operator, or regulator — each values different skills
